# IELTS Exam Format and Scoring

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) tests four skills: Listening, Reading, Writing and Speaking. Total test time is about 2 hours 45 minutes. Listening, Reading and Writing are taken in one sitting; Speaking may be on the same day or up to a week before/after. There are two modules: **Academic** (university admission, professional registration) and **General Training** (migration, work experience, training programmes). Listening and Speaking are identical in both modules; Reading and Writing differ.

## Listening (30 minutes + 10 minutes transfer time on paper-based)

- 40 questions, 4 parts, recording played ONCE only.
- **Part 1**: a conversation between two people in an everyday social context (e.g. booking accommodation). Typically form completion.
- **Part 2**: a monologue in an everyday social context (e.g. a talk about local facilities). Often map/plan labelling or multiple choice.
- **Part 3**: a conversation between up to four people in an educational/training context (e.g. students discussing an assignment).
- **Part 4**: a monologue on an academic subject (e.g. a university lecture). Often note completion, no pause in the middle.
- Question types: multiple choice, matching, plan/map/diagram labelling, form completion, note completion, table completion, flow-chart completion, sentence completion, short-answer questions.
- Each correct answer = 1 mark. Spelling and word limits are enforced. Difficulty increases from Part 1 to Part 4.

## Reading (60 minutes, no transfer time)

- 40 questions. Each correct answer = 1 mark.
- **Academic**: 3 long passages (~2,150-2,750 words in total) from books, journals, magazines and newspapers, written for a non-specialist audience; at least one contains detailed logical argument.
- **General Training**: Section 1 — two/three short everyday texts (notices, advertisements); Section 2 — two workplace texts (job descriptions, staff handbooks); Section 3 — one longer descriptive/instructive passage of general interest.
- Question types: multiple choice, identifying information (True/False/Not Given), identifying writer's views/claims (Yes/No/Not Given), matching information, matching headings, matching features, matching sentence endings, sentence completion, summary/note/table/flow-chart completion, diagram label completion, short-answer questions.
- Recommended pacing: 20 minutes per passage; passages increase in difficulty.

## Writing (60 minutes)

- **Task 1** (20 minutes recommended, minimum 150 words):
  - Academic: describe, summarise or explain visual information — line graph, bar chart, pie chart, table, process diagram, map, or a combination.
  - General Training: write a letter (formal, semi-formal or informal) requesting information or explaining a situation.
- **Task 2** (40 minutes recommended, minimum 250 words): a discursive essay in response to a point of view, argument or problem. Both modules; General Training topics are slightly more general-interest.
- Task 2 contributes twice as much as Task 1 to the Writing band.
- Assessed on: Task Achievement/Response, Coherence and Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy (25% each).

## Speaking (11-14 minutes, face-to-face with an examiner, recorded)

- **Part 1 — Introduction and interview (4-5 minutes)**: identity check, then questions on familiar topics (home, work, studies, hobbies), usually 3 topic frames.
- **Part 2 — Long turn (3-4 minutes)**: candidate receives a cue card with a topic and 3-4 bullet prompts, has 1 minute to prepare (paper and pencil provided), then speaks for 1-2 minutes; the examiner may ask one or two rounding-off questions.
- **Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 minutes)**: abstract, two-way discussion of issues linked to the Part 2 topic (comparisons, speculation, evaluation).
- Assessed on: Fluency and Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy, Pronunciation (25% each).

## Band Scores

Bands run from 0 ("did not attempt") to 9 ("expert user") in half-band steps. The **Overall Band Score** is the mean of the four skill bands, rounded to the nearest half band; .25 rounds UP to the next half band and .75 rounds UP to the next whole band (e.g. 6.125 → 6.0; 6.25 → 6.5; 6.75 → 7.0).

Band summaries: 9 expert user; 8 very good user (occasional unsystematic inaccuracies); 7 good user (operational command, occasional inaccuracies); 6 competent user (generally effective command despite inaccuracies); 5 modest user (partial command, copes with overall meaning); 4 limited user (basic competence limited to familiar situations).

## Key practical rules

- Answers in Listening/Reading must respect stated word limits ("NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS AND/OR A NUMBER"); exceeding the limit = wrong.
- Misspelled answers are marked wrong in Listening and Reading. Both British and American spellings are accepted.
- There is no negative marking — never leave a blank.
- On computer-delivered IELTS, Listening has 2 minutes checking time instead of 10 minutes transfer time.
- Writing answers must be in full sentences for Task 2; notes or bullet points are penalised.
- IELTS scores are typically valid for 2 years for most institutions.
