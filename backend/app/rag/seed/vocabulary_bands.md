# Vocabulary for IELTS Bands 7-9: Lexical Resource Guidance

The Lexical Resource descriptors reward: range, precision, "less common lexical items", collocation, awareness of style, and successful paraphrase. Band 7 requires "less common lexical items with some awareness of style and collocation"; Band 8 requires using them "skilfully" with only occasional inaccuracy. Inaccurate use of ambitious vocabulary scores LOWER than accurate use of mid-level vocabulary — precision beats decoration.

## Basic → Advanced Upgrades (use accurately and naturally)

| Basic | Higher-band alternatives |
|---|---|
| good | beneficial, advantageous, favourable, valuable, worthwhile |
| bad | detrimental, harmful, adverse, damaging, counterproductive |
| big | substantial, considerable, significant, extensive, vast |
| small | minimal, negligible, marginal, modest, slight |
| important | crucial, vital, essential, paramount, indispensable |
| a lot of | a great deal of, numerous, a considerable number of, an abundance of |
| get | obtain, acquire, gain, derive, attain |
| show | demonstrate, illustrate, indicate, reveal, reflect |
| think | maintain, contend, argue, believe, hold the view that |
| problem | issue, challenge, obstacle, drawback, shortcoming |
| solve | address, tackle, resolve, mitigate, alleviate, remedy |
| help | facilitate, assist, foster, promote, support |
| stop | prevent, deter, curb, prohibit, discourage |
| change | transform, alter, modify, shift, revolutionise |
| use | utilise, employ, exploit, harness, draw on |
| very + adj | use a stronger single adjective: very tired → exhausted; very important → crucial; very poor → destitute |
| people | individuals, citizens, members of the public, the population, residents |
| more and more | increasingly, a growing number of, an ever-increasing proportion of |

## Collocation (the Band 7 gatekeeper)

Correct collocation signals genuine range. High-value academic collocations:
- **make**: make progress, make a decision, make a contribution, make an effort, make a distinction
- **take**: take measures, take responsibility, take advantage of, take into account, take precedence
- **verb + noun**: pose a threat, raise awareness, bridge the gap, place a burden on, meet the demand, address the issue, play a pivotal role, exert an influence, reach a consensus, strike a balance, broaden one's horizons
- **adjective + noun**: a pressing issue, a viable solution, a lasting impact, a heated debate, disposable income, sedentary lifestyle, sustainable development, a vicious cycle, an ageing population, cutting-edge technology
- **adverb + adjective/verb**: widely regarded, deeply rooted, heavily reliant, readily available, mutually beneficial, environmentally friendly, rise dramatically, differ significantly

Common collocation ERRORS to avoid: "make a research" (do/conduct research), "do a decision" (make), "big possibility" (strong possibility), "take attention" (pay attention), "raise a problem" (raise an issue / pose a problem), "solve the pollution" (tackle/reduce pollution).

## Topic Vocabulary Clusters (frequent IELTS themes)

**Environment**: carbon emissions, renewable/fossil-fuel energy, deforestation, biodiversity loss, ecological footprint, climate mitigation, single-use plastics, conservation efforts, environmental degradation, sustainable practices.

**Education**: curriculum, compulsory schooling, tertiary/higher education, vocational training, rote learning vs critical thinking, academic attainment, extracurricular activities, tuition fees, lifelong learning, pedagogical approaches.

**Technology**: automation, artificial intelligence, digital literacy, remote working, screen time, data privacy, technological advancement, obsolete, breakthrough, the digital divide.

**Health**: sedentary lifestyle, obesity epidemic, preventive healthcare, mental well-being, balanced diet, life expectancy, chronic illness, healthcare provision, public health campaigns.

**Society/Government**: urbanisation, infrastructure, income inequality, social cohesion, welfare provision, taxation, legislation, public expenditure, standard of living, demographic shift.

**Work**: job satisfaction, work-life balance, remuneration, career prospects, workforce, redundancy, flexible working arrangements, employee retention, gig economy.

**Crime**: deterrent, rehabilitation, reoffending/recidivism, custodial sentence, juvenile delinquency, law enforcement, capital punishment, community service.

## Less Common / Idiomatic Lexis (Speaking Band 7+)

Natural idiomatic language for Speaking (use sparingly, only where it fits): "a double-edged sword", "over the moon", "a stone's throw away", "burn the midnight oil", "keep an eye on", "food for thought", "in the long run", "off the beaten track", "part and parcel of", "second to none", "broaden my horizons", "get out of my comfort zone", "time-consuming", "eye-opening". Forced or misused idioms lower the score — one natural idiom beats three awkward ones.

## Linking Phrase Inventory (Writing)

- **Introducing views**: It is widely believed that; A common argument is that; Proponents/Opponents of X claim that; From my perspective.
- **Adding**: Furthermore; Moreover; In addition to this; Not only... but also; Equally important is.
- **Contrasting**: However; Nevertheless; Conversely; While it is true that..., ...; Despite this; On the contrary (only to contradict a negative claim).
- **Cause and effect**: Consequently; As a result; This, in turn, leads to; Owing to; stems from; gives rise to.
- **Exemplifying**: For instance; A case in point is; This is exemplified by; Take X, for example.
- **Conceding**: Admittedly; Granted; Although there is some merit in this view; It must be acknowledged that.
- **Concluding**: In conclusion; To sum up; On balance; Taking everything into consideration.

## Paraphrase Techniques (tested everywhere in IELTS)

1. **Synonyms**: children → youngsters/minors; buy → purchase; help → assistance (watch register and exact meaning).
2. **Word form change**: pollute → pollution → polluted; benefit (n) → beneficial (adj).
3. **Voice change**: "The government should fund schools" → "Schools should be funded by the state".
4. **Structure change**: "Because cities are crowded" → "Owing to urban overcrowding".
5. **Antonym + negation**: expensive → not affordable / far from cheap.

## Register warnings

Avoid in formal writing: kids (children), a lot of (a great deal of), stuff/things (aspects, factors), gonna/wanna, really/so + adj (highly, considerably), "you" for people in general (individuals, one), contractions (do not, it is), phrasal verbs where a single verb is more formal: go up → increase, find out → discover, put off → postpone, look into → investigate, get rid of → eliminate. In Speaking, however, natural phrasal verbs and informal lexis are appropriate and rewarded.

## Spelling and word formation (Bands 7-9)

Errors must be rare and non-impeding. High-frequency IELTS misspellings to master: government, environment, accommodation, advertisement, beneficial, definitely, necessary, occurred, percentage, receive, society, technology, tendency, particularly, opportunity. Word-formation traps: economic (of the economy) vs economical (cheap to use); affect (verb) vs effect (noun); rise (intransitive) vs raise (transitive); its vs it's.
